How the game works

The mafia game is a group activity where players are assigned roles as villagers or mafia. The game consists of night and day phases. During the night, the mafia chooses a villager to kill, and the detective can investigate someone’s mafia status. In the day phase, the villagers discuss and vote on who they suspect is part of the mafia. The game ends when either all mafia members are eliminated (villagers win) or all non-mafia players are killed (mafia wins). The detective’s role adds strategic elements to the voting process. The assigned roles are hidden, allowing anyone to claim to be the detective, making it challenging for the real detective to navigate their role. The question that we'll try to answer with this model is: How does the incorporation of epistemic logic influence the outcome of the game of mafia?.
