Introduction

The Game of Mafia

Mafia is a strategy game about a battle between two distinct factions. On one side, the Mafia forms a minority but is equipped with the knowledge of who the other mafiosi are. They share a common goal to eliminate the Townfolk. The Townfolk, comprising an unaware majority, must navigate the deceit of the mafia and strategise to identify and eliminate the hidden Mafia members, initially having no other knowledge than their own role.
The game is split into two types of phases: day and night. During the night phase, the mafia members carefully select a villager to be eliminated from the game. Moreover, there are various other special roles [1], most of which also can perform their special ability during the night phase. Take for instance the often used detective, who is able to inspect any player to get to know their role.
In the day phase, all players come together to discuss and debate. Through deliberation and analysis of behavioral cues, the players engage in a democratic voting process to determine who they suspect of being part of the mafia. The mafia, logically, tries to deceive the townfolk and to not have anyone part of their faction voted out.
The game concludes when one of two conditions is met: either all mafia members are successfully eliminated, resulting in a victory for the villagers, or the mafia manages to eliminate all non-mafia players, securing a win for themselves.

Our Project

Strategic thinking takes center stage throughout the game. The Mafia carefully selects their victims while trying to manipulate the Townfolk's perceptions. Meanwhile, the Townfolk strategize during the day phase, leveraging social interactions, arguments, and alliances to build a consensus on who they believe to be part of the Mafia. One could argue that the more knowledge the players have, the better they are able to navigate through the different phases and achieve the goal of eliminating the other faction. Our project will focus on modelling the game of mafia in a way where agents, like in the real game, can use conversations and deductive reasoning to find out the roles of other players. In doing so, we hope to answer the following:
Research Question. How does the incorporation of epistemic logic influence the outcome of the game of mafia?
